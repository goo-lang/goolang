M7-stdlib-expansion was gated on three foundational compiler bugs, not stdlib content. The gpu_intrinsic/sync/alloc/copy grammar rules lived in primary_expr matching any ident.ident shape and hijacked every package-method call (fmt.Println parsed as a CUDA blockIdx.x intrinsic; fmt.Println() parsed as gpu_sync and failed with 'Unknown GPU sync function'). codegen_get_basic_type returned NULL for TYPE_STRING — the broader codegen_type_to_llvm had a case for it, but the basic-type variant didn't, causing string-literal SIGSEGVs via LLVMGetUndef(NULL). LLVMGetElementType doesn't work with LLVM 22 opaque pointers (use LLVMGlobalGetValueType for global functions). The stdlib .goo files were not the bottleneck — the type-checker→codegen→runtime wiring was. type_check_selector_expr was a 'Struct field access not yet implemented' stub; libgoo_runtime.a only bundled runtime.o and link-failed on goo_deadlock_init. Test claims of '100% pass rate' did not reflect main-branch reality.
